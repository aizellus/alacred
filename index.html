Standards Framework

Credibility backed by practical categories customers actually care about.

The review model is designed around visible proof, not vague claims. Every business is evaluated against weighted trust categories that map to how customers judge confidence.

Trust and Transparency

Published identity, service clarity, pricing approach, and complaint visibility.

Customer Experience

Lead response, appointment communication, follow-up, and resolution habits.

Operational Reliability

Scheduling discipline, quality checks, staff accountability, and documented process.

Safety and Preparedness

Safety expectations, hazard control, preparedness, and relevant coverage proof.

Community Engagement

Local participation, giving, partnerships, and authentic Alabama presence.

Digital Trust

Current website, consistent listings, responsive reviews, and understandable contact or payment flows.

FAQ

Early answers for launch conversations.

Is AlaCred a directory, an award, or a membership?

It is best understood as a standards-driven trust credential with public recognition and business visibility benefits layered on top.

What kinds of evidence are reviewed?

Evidence may include website screenshots, policy documents, contracts, reviews, training records, safety material, and operational proof.

Who is it best suited for first?

Reputation-sensitive local businesses where trust meaningfully affects conversion, including service businesses, professional firms, hospitality, and community-facing operators.

Why does the Alabama angle matter?

Local identity creates stronger affinity, better relevance, and a trust story national programs struggle to match.
